# The New York Times Adult Best Seller List - 2025

## Motivation
After noticing that most books I read claim that that are a "New York Times Best Seller",
I wanted to explore what this really means. The main question I had driving this inspection was, "How many books are New York Times Best Sellers?" To start, I explored Adult New York Times Best Sellers in the year 2025. All data used was obtained through the PDF's located at https://www.hawes.com/pastlist.htm.

## Organization of R Code
I began by writing a function that would convert the data provided in the PDF's into a single, clean, and ready to use data
frame which I named df_2025 then downloaded as a .csv file. I then moved onto the data visulizations. For each of the three
that I made, I included at the begining the code used to further subset my original data df_2025 to get different stories
out of my data. 

## Insights and Static Poster
After creating informative visuals, I put them together onto a poster to present them and my findings. There were a few key insights

1. The majority of books that make the list stay only 1 or 2 weeks (Figure 1)
2. The books spending the most weeks on the list in Fiction and Nonfiction both started at higher positions (1-15 for each category) in the begining of the year (Figure 3)
